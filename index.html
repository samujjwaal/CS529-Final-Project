MedRT

Radiation therapy is a primary modality to treat head and neck cancer patients. In this project, we propose a novel approach to correlate similarities and differences between the cancer patients. We have used the head and neck cancer patients’ data from UIC and MDACC Radiation Therapy plan and visualized the similarities and dissimilarities between them.

Distribution plot:

Data distribution chart is added for the MDACC patient dataset on the basis of patients’ tumor size, age, given dose volume, treatment duration and smoking status. We showed different encodings (i.e. box plot, violin plot, bean plot, scatter plot) to visualize data distribution and tried to know how a user might interpret these encodings. On hovering mouse over the plots, a tooltip shows the maximum, median, minimum and quartiles.

Bubble plot:

Bubble plot shows the patients’ cohorts in terms of gender, race, HPV, overall survival, T-category, tumor subsite. On hovering mouse over a certain cohort, only that cohort shows and other cohorts fade out. If a single patient is clicked, the bar chart - showing the selected patient's organs and the 3D plot change according to it.

Bar Chart:

A dorpdown is added for selecting a patient. A bar chart shows how much the patient’s organs were affected by the dose volume given to the patient.

3D plot:

For 3D view, we have plotted the organs and distributed the color range depending upon the dose amount that organ received. For one patient (selected from the dropdown), four most similar patients are showing on the right side. Hovering the mouse on the organs, a tooltip shows the organ name and dose value.
